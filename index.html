🎓 How This Model Works

1

The Problem: Traditional banks only look at PD (Probability of Default). If PD > 10%, they reject the loan. But this ignores how profitable the customer is — many profitable customers get wrongly rejected.

2

My Model: I built an AI model (Logistic Regression + Gradient Boosting) that first predicts PD from borrower features, then computes Expected Loss, Net Profit, Economic Capital, and finally RAROC.

3

RAROC is the key insight: RAROC = Net Profit ÷ Economic Capital. If RAROC ≥ 15% (hurdle rate), the loan is worth approving even if PD seems high — because interest income covers the risk.

4

The Result: My RAROC-based model approved 39.5% more customers than the traditional PD model — unlocking ₹159 Lakhs of additional profit while keeping portfolio risk under control.

5

The 4-quadrant matrix: Customers are segmented into Approve (low risk, high profit), Reprice (low risk, low profit), Price Premium (high risk, high profit), and Reject (high risk, low profit). This gives a clear action for every customer.
